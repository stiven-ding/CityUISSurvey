He’s back!

What’s new, you may ask.  What isn’t I might reply.

This is a fresh adventure that is made for characters of strength, ready for those characters who are nearing retirement after a lifetime of adventure.   

All those tales of woe at the hands of The Forgotten Dungeon.  A worthy party returns to rehash memories and walk the hallways of a familiar old dungeon they helped clear out.  Only, the dungeon is alive…again…  There is danger.  Do the ole souls have it in them for one last adventure before they hang up the battle axe upon the mantle of their fireplace?  Questions.  If you want the answers, bring those ole souls forward and find out for yourselves.

There’s a bar.  Come have a drink.  There’s a stage.  Come watch a play.  There’s a jail.  Watch your step.  There’s a torture chamber.  Uh oh.  There’s a wizard’s tower.  Double uh oh.  There’s danger…a plenty.  Are you sure you’re ready? Fantasy Levels 11+

-Edward Jonas

This Kickstarter will produce three new adventures in the Geneva Cavern Adventure series which began with The Forgotten Dungeon and Mirror Mirror. These adventures were designed for use with D&D 5e and also include stats for 1e, 77 Worlds, and Dragonscales.

We will produce Return to The Forgotten Dungeon and the "Beyond the Forgotten Dungeon" Adventures Pyramid of Doom and Lair of an Evil god. In addition, we will produce the Geneva Cavern Map and map booklet and the Monster Folio.

The Pyramid of Doom! (Beyond the Forgotten Dungeon)

Looking out from the exit of The Forgotten Dungeon is a vast cavern and, down from them and in the distance, a pyramid stands by a strip of water.  Although the land is varied and there is much to take in, they all can see where they travel next.

The Pyramid of Doom is an advanced gaming module that carries on the tradition of The Forgotten Dungeon, in that it is designed to work with four gaming systems, 1e Fantasy, 5e Fantasy, The Lost 77 Worlds (a post-apocalyptic gaming system), and DragonScales RPG.

The difficulty of this setting is on par with Return to The Forgotten Dungeon.  As the name implies, this module is no cake walk.  I’d suggest an 11th level party, if playing it in a fantasy setting.  To contrast, The Forgotten Dungeon was for an 8+ level party.

Lair of an Evil God

Meat Grinder edition

Whistling wind. Bent willow. An untiring push upon the land, clothes billowing on the line. 

Something, some deep and dark, some lurching madness, some inky darkness seeping eagerly, hungrily. You're not safe. You're not SAFE! There is no hiding, no escape from your unwise decision to shakily enter, Lair of an Evil God. Beware to your family, past friends.  Do not follow. R.I.P. 

A pop of a closing door, distant peels of hungry rippling laughter... now no more. Mundane life, returns, for the survivors; those too scared to dare enter a blisteringly twisted black hearted realm of terror, anguished, painful, unlife. Reality twists. You're unworthy, weak, pathetically unprepared.  Mad screams, tentacles, wrenching turns of a wrench along the flat road of an unholy adventure.  Best you carry on says flapping carrion...wings.  You're too weak and oh so pathetic. Your magic is wholly inadequate.  Your poor excuse for light and your party are endlessly feeble, a nothing against brutal overpowering, bulging unyielding strength.  Nurse away little child, echoes on the mind, a dance of hateful thoughts; blood bath.

Pyramid of Doom!!  Lair of an Evil God.
