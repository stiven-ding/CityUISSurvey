 Introduction 

 DINO BOT is a sole ownership start-up app game development company based in Milwaukee Wisconsin. DINO BOT is seeking funding to launch an app game titled “Death Cloud". “Death Cloud" will be a live stream battle Royale open reality game offered to the public as a downloadable freemium app. 

“Death Cloud" is a full-service creative technology firm that will launch this app game concept and design it with an expert in-house programming team. “Death Cloud" will launch in the app stores for all mobile iOS and Android phones and digital portable devices (iPad). 

DINO BOT is releasing a game that will leverage on the marketplace's popular and profitable existing 1st and 3rd person shooter games. "Death Cloud" will be launched on all major app seller platforms where people are already consuming digital entertainment and purchasing app games in large volumes. "Death Cloud" will be released as a free downloadable app, with in-app purchase offerings for player options, weaponry upgrades and modifications, etc. 




"Death Cloud" Game Description 

"Death Cloud" Game Description - A 1st person shooting game. The game will consist of 5 states each with five cities.  Each city has 10 missions, including 10 side missions.  The user has to complete all the missions in a certain city to unlock the next city.  Players can acquire coins by waiting over a specific period of time by playing the freemium version of the game, watching ads to accrue coins, and being able to also directly make purchases in the shop with coin or currency via digital payment transactions. The game will achieving broad appeal with aspects of the game design and storyline that are engaging to the hardcore gamers, while other features appeal to the casual gamer. The game will be made easy to share via existing mobile social networks. 

Players don't have to pay anything to unlock the full game !  Every board that a player completes unlocks a new board, we're not charging them for more maps or boards! We are only charging them for any in-app purchases they might make, and we also collect 5% on any gaming fee when they participate in via the game tournaments. 

The business model we have created to produce profits via “Death Cloud” sales is broken down as follows: 

We will offer “Death Cloud” as a Free-to-Play (F2P or FTP) game - free to initially download, but requiring payment for various upgrades. 

Freemium games are free to download and play but offer small in-game purchases known as micro-transactions. The top grossing mobile games in 2018 have all been freemium model games such as Clash Royale and Fortnight Battle Royale. 

Micro-transactions allow players to purchase additional virtual goods like cosmetic skins, exclusive gear (e.g. armor, shields), loot boxes, or in-game currency. In-game purchases vary - some enhance the player’s power or speed up their progress within the game, while others are entirely cosmetic. An example of a micro-transaction for a roleplaying game (RPG) is a player purchasing a limited-edition potion which gives their character special powers that would be otherwise very difficult or time- consuming to obtain via normal gameplay.

Our strategy is to use the Freemium Free-to-Play (F2P or FTP) model to allow consumers to play a free version of the “Death Cloud” game, but require payment to unlock the full game features and digital accessories. 

The Freemium Free-to-Play model gives users limited gameplay compared to the full game — the idea is to entice users to pay for the game upgrades once they’ve gotten a chance to play the game. We also offer free gameplay that can be rewarded by awarding players different game features that are unlocked at various stages of accrued hours that each player plays on the game while watching advertisements at various pauses of game play and stage advancement. This creates a mechanism to generate advertising revenue from a captive audience of game players who even though they are playing for free can be monetized by the audience watching advertisements in the game. 

Our secondary strategy for revenue stream will use the sale of advertising space within the game. We can offer the game free, but run advertisements as a means of revenue.  Share of advertising dollars as part of publisher revenue continues to grow - with the top of the digital charts dominated by a shrinking number of top firms, the industry's middle class turns to advertising to pay its rent.  Second-tier publishers will rely on ads for about 15%-20% of annual revenue, compared to 70%+ for smaller outfits.The following types of advertisement placement within the "Death Cloud" game are as follows: 

Display ads are the most standard type, which can include static ads, dynamic display ads, banner ads, video ads, and pop-up ads. Occasionally inserts are dynamic display ads as a small banner at the bottom of the screen. 

Interstitial ads are ads that are shown automatically in intervals. Interstitial ads are common for games with a level-up progression since they can be displayed between levels where there is a natural break in the gameplay, making them relatively not intrusive. For example, requiring the player to watch a 30-second video after beating a level. 

Incentivized ads offer an in-game incentive, like an in-game currency or a boost, in exchange for interacting with an ad (e.g. watching a video ad). Puzzle game Candy Crush will offer to give users a bonus if they watch a short video before beginning a new level. 

Contextual ads are integrated into gameplay, often as branded gameplay objects or branded storefronts within the scenery of a game. Think branded Pokestops in Pokemon Go or billboards on the streets of Grand Theft Auto. 

Another avenue for revenue is branded merchandise as another outlet to bring in revenue. Gaming fans who will purchase t-shirts and collectible merchandise as an extension of their love of the game.

The mobile gaming marketplace is very competitive market, naturally favoring incumbents with large distribution networks, world-class performance marketing structure, large amounts of capital, access to big brands, ability to create really high monetizing products supported with massive marketing spends. The Team at Dino Bot has created a launch strategy based on the risks and challenges we assed in our S.W.O.T. There used to be a distinction between gamers and non-gamers. You were either a consumer who bought a console device, spending hundreds of dollars up front and $50 a pop to buy those games, or you were a consumer who could not afford to buy a game console, nor the additional game purchases. The current mobile gaming market has done away with that distinction. Everyone has the potential to be a gamer because everybody has a gaming device with them all of the time via their mobile devices. That includes people who were traditionally PC gamers who are now playing more games on their touchscreen devices. This includes consumers who were relatively casual gamers are becoming more engaged via mobile gaming platforms. The mobile gaming trend is catering to both core game players who now play games on mobile devices, and people who were casual gamers becoming comfortable with deeper gameplay via the ease of access to gaming titles. The advent of touchscreens may have triggered that sort of convergence, but Driver said there's more to come on the hardware side. Products like Apple TV bringing mobile experiences to the living room can only further erode the distinctions people have about what types of games are appropriate for what type of hardware. The distinction between gamer and non-gamer has become nonexistent. The distinction between console gaming and mobile gaming will become less distinct as the mobile gaming market continues to mature.
