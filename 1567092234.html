Story

Is your commute less than ideal? Are other commuters pushing you around?

Ever wonder what it would be like to push back...or maybe do a little bit more?

Or maybe A LOT more?

Krazy Train allows you to do just that! In this 2D, side-scrolling, action game for Android and iOS. Become our hero who just wants to get to work on time. Kill off, dodge, and avoid all sorts of evil commuters. Use your briefcase, as well as other street items you can pick up and store. Craft better weapons by combining items you've collected. Gather sustenance to maintain life and energy. Do what you can to get to avoid being tardy. Better yet, don't die! 

In 2016, Krazy Train was started by a small team of gamers. Later in the year, they submitted the game to PlayStation Portugal's Premios Contest where it made the Top-10 list. They were invited to exhibit the game inside PlayStation's extravagant booth at Lisbon Games Week 2016 (the largest gaming convention in Portugal).

It was considered for two awards that year, and many of the thousands of gamers that got a chance to play a test level of Krazy Train were excited, amused, and even mildly offended by its campy and dark humor.  

But when the game failed to garner any top awards, momentum from the team dissipated and the project was put on hold for almost two years.

Since then, two of the members started Saltie Games LLC with other like-minded, motivated individuals where they have revitalized Krazy Train from a PS4 platform to the mobile platform it was intended for, giving it the best possible gaming experience.

They want to ensure that Krazy Train's development, marketing, programming, and user acquisition is optimized so that the potential for success is at its highest.

Risks and challenges

The main risks are serious development issues with the third-party development team we will be working with (who will remain anonymous), including costs that may be incurred from unforeseen technical issues that can run us over or out of budget. The risk of this happening is very-low to medium-low. If costs do run us out of budget, the project will still be completed, but at a much longer production time. Other, even less likely risks are an unsuccessful soft launch, which will require user acquisition retooling and an additional soft launch. Our Project Manager and Game Designer has extensive experience with mobile game UA and though no game is guaranteed to profit, we are guaranteeing at least a full launch.
