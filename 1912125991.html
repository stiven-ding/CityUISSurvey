What's in a Name?

Jeau D'eau (literally "Water Game") references the French composer Maurice Ravel's piano piece which seeks to imitate water in all its forms - spraying, trickling, splashing, crashing. This term also alludes to the magnificent water fountains in Versailles and other parts of France where architects sought to seamlessly incorporate moving water as a motif into royal gardens. These beautiful images of Fluidity, Movement, and Creation are all central to the heart of card play, whether used for games, flourishing or magic. 

Old Joke

We dig the past. Well, not as much as archeologists, but we really do love old books. For our joker cards we decided to lift vintage art straight off of an old card magic book in our Archive from 1902 (a great year for card books). The sleek, dark pen-and-ink drawings work perfectly with the project as a whole. Have a look for yourself.

These historic images of the Victorian magician reminded us of the vintage theatre magic posters put on display in times past. We decided to pay tribute to this lost era and create our very own intimate 11x17 version. Frame yours and hang it in any Academy or Edwardian Smoking Room.

Please note: Only thirty (30) mini posters will be available, plus an additional seven (7) set aside to be autographed by the Ravenswood team (scroll down to meet us). We want the poster in your hall or video to be a novel and rare sight for the curious onlooker, no matter how well traveled they are.

"Who's going to print your cards?" That depends on how many deck orders we receive during this campaign as some suppliers will take small orders (100 decks), others require larger orders (1000). 

We understand that this question comes from concern over the quality of the finished product (and rightly so); Here's what we can promise:

1. There will be Air Pockets. 

Want to see what we mean? Let's zoom in on one of our card corners.

       See the indentions? These are air pockets. Air pockets mean less friction as less of the card is in contact with the others. This means smooth ribbon spreads, fans, riffle shuffles and overall fine handling when the stakes are high. 

      We have heard horror stories of cards ordered online only to arrive virtually useless: no air pockets means a virtually un-fannable deck. Trust us: we want to play with these cards. There will be air pockets.

2. The cards you will receive will handle just as well as the ones in the above video. We ordered the same deck from different suppliers to compare handling. The one we used for filming happened to feature Casino Quality card stock (and air pockets!), sourced from France. We realize the preference in thickness and slickness of cards varies from cardist to cardist, but ultimately we will deliver a deck that will keep up with all your shuffling skills.
