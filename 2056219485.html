What is MULTI?

MULTI is a fantastic 2-player math game designed with the joy of mathematics in mind. This game is so fun your kids won't want to stop playing, and neither will you! 

The game was originally designed for students in 2nd-5th grade to develop a deeper understanding of multiplication, multiples, and factors. However, over the years as more people had a chance to play the game, the rich strategy and gameplay have proven seriously fun for people of all ages!

In this multidimensional take on tic-tac-toe, players must harness the structure of multiples and factors, as they play on 9 small tic tac toe boards simultaneously in hopes of winning 3 small boards in a row on the big board!

Why the world needs Multi

Mathematics is a polarizing topic! For some, it is a fountain of wonder, beauty, and intrigue. For others, it is a cold dark thing, something to be avoided or even feared. Yet, my experience has shown that everyone can find joy in mathematics when it is presented in the right way!

Perhaps no single topic in elementary mathematics education has been more mistreated than multiplication. The antiquated and ill-informed focus on memorization and speed has single-handedly contributed to millions of young students developing math anxiety. Many can identify with this need for a different way to learn about multiplication but do not know where to begin.

Multi is a game that was invented to provide a joyful and playful context for which to explore multiplication, multiples, and factors. Not only does it accomplish the goal of developing fluency with multiplication facts, more importantly, it also teaches about the structure and relationship between multiples and factors.

Using The Factor Board To Place Xs and Os:

On the first turn, the first player places a token on a number on the Factor Board and places Xs on each square that has that number.

In the example below, the 1st player placed the token on 4 and places Xs on all the 4s on the Multi Board. 

Player 2 then places the second token on a number in the Factor Board and places an O on each square that has the product of the two numbers.

In the example below, the 2nd player has placed the second token on 6 and places Os on all 24s because 6x4=24.

In each subsequent turn players must move one of the two tokens to claim the squares corresponding with the product of the two numbers with tokens.

In the example below, the 1st player has moved the 6 to the 3 and places Xs on all 12s because 4x3=12.

Placing a Large X or O

When you get 3 in a row in a small tic-tac-toe board replace the small Xs/Os with a large X/O.

Some Special Circumstances:

  • If a tic-tac-toe game inside a large square ends in a draw (Cats Game), the large square becomes wild after the last square is taken (Counts for X and O)
  • If a player can not make a legal move, they lose their turn
