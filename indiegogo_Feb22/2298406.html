We need your help to fund our debut album! We've been hard at work cultivating our own sound on 100% handmade, upcycled instruments. We are proud to promote sustainable practices and self-sufficiency while spreading a positive message with our music.

"This Band Sounds Like Garbage!"

Indeed, we summon musical tones and rhythms out of upcycled materials, thrift shop treasures, and long lost relics. All our instruments are homemade: you'll have a hard time finding anything like them in a music store. Pot lid xylophones, tin "canjos", diddley bows, cigar box guitars, dresser drawer bass and a whole suitcase of found sounds. We specialize in our own concoction of New Orleans swing and traditional jazz, with hints of gypsy jazz, Brazilian choro, and funk. 

Humble Beginnings of a Trashtronaut

This journey of mine began a handful of years ago in Tallahassee. I had a vision to combine teaching music, physics of sound, DIY building, and sustainability all into one package: I called it Trashtronauts. For the last 4 years, I've made it a priority to learn and teach sustainable practices by building musical instruments out of upcycled materials and inspiring others to think similarly. I've hosted instrument building workshops, community drum circles, and more! If you want to learn more, our website is 

Trashtronauts: http://soundslikegarbage.org

Way Down Yonder...

Two years ago, I moved to New Orleans to pursue more musical opportunities. I started a band called the Cigar Box Serenaders and featured more jazz and swing music. We've been playing shows around New Orleans for the last year but it's time for us to make a musical statement with the recording of our debut album. This album will help our sound waves reach the ears of many that don't have the chance to make it down to NOLA as well as expand our musical audience here in the city. We're confident that our fresh creative approach to playing music will bring a smile and cheer to those who lend an ear.

Get Free Stuff!

In exchange for your generosity, I'll be working hard to make you things! Over the last few years, I've been fascinated by primitive and handmade instruments. I've spent my free time researching and building what I can, and now it's my time to share them with you. As well as a digital or physical copy of the album, you can receive:

  • One-string canjo and "Easy Way To Play Canjo" book with 20 play-by-numbers songs
  • Cigar box guitars, bottleneck slide canjos, and cigar box sitars
  • Custom windchimes

If we earn more money than our goal, we will be able to record a longer album with more songs for you!
