Chronicles of the Wayfarer, Heroes is a fast-paced tabletop skirmish game. Small warbands adventure into perilous situations led by heroes questing in the lands of Valhier to accomplish what may be impossible or impractical for their armies. Only their wit and loyal henchmen assist them as they face the dangers that lie before them to complete their missions. The lands of Valhier have transformed, and the clock of a new age has begun. Thousands of years have passed since the Splintering, and the developing races have never known a time when the Illock did not exist. This nebulous cloud that surrounds their continent is lethal to those who enter. And yet the bravest and foolish still attempt to survey and harvest the treasure which lies within the Illock to their great peril: Pantherium and its magical power.

What makes the design of Chronicles of the Wayfarer distinct is the emphasis on freedom of individual model choice. Whether it is moving, fighting, or interacting while attempting to complete asymmetrical objectives, you will decide every aspect of your models’ actions and how hard to push them each turn. Players will alternate activations to assure nobody has an absolute initiative advantage. Your ability to make tactical decisions on the fly will determine how well your warband performs and ultimately, the outcome of the scenario. Valhier is an unforgiving and deadly world, but you can adapt your characters, strategies, and equipment to take on your challengers head on. 


If you don’t care for hulking around scores of figures and you are looking for a good balance between tactical play, character development, and narrative play, then Chronicles of the Wayfarer, Heroes might be the game for you. We embarked on this journey with a few core ambitions. One ambition was to make a game with unique figures that you can grow attached to as you play, maximizing the creative, emotional, and financial investment you make into each of the characters. We also wanted to make it easy to learn and a challenge to master. Our goals included making it as fun for the defeated as for the victor.

With your rules pledge, you will receive the rules, character cards, and counters. We will offer options for a digital PDF and hard copy version for those who want one, but we would recommend waiting until we have had the digital version out for a bit to help uncover anything glaring. We also look forward to feedback as to what we can improve before producing the expanded softback product. Anyone who purchases this version will have access to all real-time updates and changes. As a thank you for being an early adopter and contributor to the rules, you will also receive a one-time discount code  for your full digital purchase to use when we release the expanded version. We will be expanding the starting factions as well as adding a few more.

In 2016, our son, who has Down syndrome, graduated from high school. Most of the options for the disabled are limited, so we decided to create our own opportunities for him. My wife left her career, and we started Boyd's Toys. We are a small, family-run business that specializes in 3D printing, resin cast figures, and commercial printing. We do our 3D printing and mold making as well as vacuum- and pressure-casting. John Boyd has over 30 years of printing and design experience, and his favorite pastime is tabletop games. We started creating our own rules and figures in resin over three years ago on a 15 mm project called Chronicles of the Wayfarer, War.

Today we are releasing our second Kickstarter: Chronicles of the Wayfarer, Heroes. Heroes is a 32 mm skirmish miniatures game. We have completed over 75 figures, created master casts for metal molds of all our figures, written the rules, prepared everything for production including over 100 pieces of professional, full-color art. All that remains is production molds and a few more family fights about the lore. We hope you will join us on this journey and we can expand this world into something unique.

Why Kickstarter?

We are bringing this project to Kickstarter for the exposure as well as to help increase our fan base. To date, our project has been completely self-funded and we have removed virtually all the risk. We already have a great fan base that has been following along with our progress. The money we collect is only to help with the production molds, everything else we have already completed and made the financial investment. We chose to do it this way so that we could expedite the product and minimize the wait and frustration we see with so many other projects. 

Shipping

Shipping fees will be calculated with the Pledge Manager and charged after the Kickstarter completes. Due to the fact we do not know how much metal each person will want, we cannot predict a universal shipping cost. In the US, we will be using USPS priority shipping so the cost should be reasonable. Overseas orders will be more expensive. We will only charge you for the packaging and actual shipping costs, and we will try to help mitigate any additional fees.


Risks and challenges

We've successfully run and managed one Kickstarter before as well as helped another maker salvage theirs, and we recognize the amount of effort required to make them work. Timing for the production molds will be our greatest challenge. We are estimating right at 4 months. As with all things in life, I am sure something will require a little extra time. We have already removed most of the delay items, so we are confident we can hit our delivery. We will commit to communicating well and letting you know what is going on, even if it is nothing new. Most of the expense has already been managed so there will not be an instance of us being under-budgeted or financially over-extended. We are posting the ship date as February of 2020. Although we anticipate being done sooner, we do not want to be responsible for missed holiday items.
