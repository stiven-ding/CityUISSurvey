Portugal is the Westernmost country in Europe. With a coastline that spans over 700km facing the Atlantic Ocean, Portugal uses well-traveled shipping routes in order to develop its industry.

The Portuguese mainland is a crucial geographical presence for the two autonomous island regions — Madeira and the Açores. Most of the exports from these regions are directed to the mainland. While the sea routes for tourism and goods play a role of great importance to the livelihood of these islands, they also offer luxury products of superb quality to the European continent as well.

The rules for the Railways of Portugal expansion (designed for 2, 3 or 4 players) are the same as those of Railways of the World, with a few important exceptions as noted below.

What's New & Different about the Railways of Portugal expansion?  

Here are the highlights:

  • Islands off-shore which receive more goods as the game goes on, but require more money to access via Maritime Connections
  • No black cities on the board, they are only added at the start of the Industrial Age and via Urbanization
  • A "tight" map (close cities) that demands careful and tough decisions which lead to significant player interaction
  • Major Lines that require intermediate connections
  • New Operations cards and concepts including: Government Subsidies, State Treasury Shares, Maritime State Support & Public-Private Partnerships
  • Goal Cards instead of Baron Cards

Ian O’Toole is an Irish illustrator, graphic designer and gamer working out of Perth in Western Australia. Since its publication as Railroad Tycoon in 2005, Railways of the World (re-named for title licensing reasons in 2009) has been constantly ranked in the Top 100 of the all-time best games on BoardGameGeek.com.  Fourteen years and over 10,000 ratings since then, it has been ranked as high as #22 and it is currently #71 (in Strategy Games) with a 7.7 BGG rating--even after all of these years. Revisit the early days of the Age of Steam as you begin with no money, a locomotive (the venerable John Bull, the first locomotive to run in North America) and a vision (your Baron's "Goal" card). From there, build your budding railroad network into a vast empire. If you are playing the Eastern US map, you can connect New York to Chicago, earn the most money, develop bigger and faster locomotives and maybe even combine expansions and span North America to build the Transcontinental Railway! 

Railways of the World  (ROTW) is the base game for the system and includes all the components necessary to play any ROTW series games with up to 6 players, including: 24 engine placards, 231 track tiles, 100 locomotives, 125 supply cubes, money, bonds, empty city markers, scoring disks, etc.  

Two game boards are included: one, depicting the Eastern half of the United States (Railways of the Eastern U.S.), is best for 4-6 players; and the other, Railways of Mexico, is best for 2-3 players.

Railways of Nippon (RON) is a 2 to 4 player version of Railways of the World  and includes a map of Japan designed by the superstar Japanese game designer Hisashi Hayashi. Railways of Nippon also includes all the components needed to play all the expansions in the ROTW Series, including Railways of Portugal.
