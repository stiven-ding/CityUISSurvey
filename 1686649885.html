Story

The Nuadan Chronicles is an electro fantasy RPG set in the post-post-apocalyptic world of Nuada, a place of ephemeral magic, electric-powered steam tech, ancient ruins, and dark secrets. Supporting the game is a modified d100 system.

Within the Known World, you are a mercenary navigating the edge between life and death, entangled within an invisible web of fate. No matter the mission, the consequences of your decisions will ripple across the many futures. Will you struggle against the tides of destiny or be consumed by the darkness lurking within?

To fight for wealth, glory or justice, you must survive an industrializing and expanding world plagued by war, espionage, disasters (natural and magical), and ancient conspiracies as old as the Cataclysm itself.

The Nuadan Chronicles will feature two books: a rulebook (~250 pages) and a lore book (~250 pages). Both will contain high-quality, full-color artwork and maps, short stories, and mission seeds.

 THE CATACLYSM

More than seven centuries have passed since the world was reborn. The old writings that survive recall a great light in the sky, followed by a powerful shockwave. Those who did not go blind watched as the second moon cracked apart, fiery comets of death raining down from the void.

Then the world darkened. Choking dust and black storms clogged the sky. The oceans rose, consuming coastal cities, then regions, then entire landmasses. Nuada grew cold. Most of the technology people relied on stopped working.

What the falling shards of broken moon and flooding did not kill, starvation, disease, and despair claimed next. The several years of suffering and survival thereafter would be known by various cultures as the Grey Years, the Long Night, and the Aftermath.

The Cataclysm split time. BC, Before the Cataclysm. This is the Lost Age, a grandiose age of prosperity, avarice, and technological superiority. AC, After the Cataclysm. Now is the Second Age, the seeds of new tribes, kingdoms, and empires emerging from the chaos.

 THE KNOWN WORLD

About a quarter of the world has been mapped. Everything beyond the Veil of Storms to the west, the equator to the south, the Dark Lands of the east, and the mountains of Uldris to the north is unknown.

With each campaign released, more and more secrets will be revealed. Each nation has its own hidden worlds, lost ruins, and new races. Eventually, explorers will be sent beyond the edges of the map to discover strange, new lands.

TECHNOLOGY

Memories of the Lost Age, pillaged ruins, and electric-steam technologies are fueling a new industrial age. Airships plow the skies and power stations illuminate metropolises. With each step, humanity draws closer to achieving the glories of the past!

  • Choose from a variety of tech-based weapons, armors, and gear (such as kinetic shields, power suits, arc slingers, sonic gauntlets, inferno shells, radios, tech packs, and more).
  • Level up in Tinkering to customize and improve your tech weapons.
  • Use inferno, kinetic, sonic, and storm modules to modify and boost tech weapons.

 MAGIC

Magic saturates the world of Nuada, defying the laws of reality even as it exists alongside it.

  •  Flux crystals, magic incarnate, permeate the earth. They are prized for their ability to store spells for later or crushed and heated to power the engines of industry.
  •  Rifts, great tears in the fabric of the physical world, lie scattered across the land and sea. They are the vast maelstroms, eternal storms, cursed deserts, sinister forests, and other phenomena many fear and few understand.
  •  Fae, immortal creatures of pure magic, roam the wilds and forgotten corners of the world, drawn to humans to satiate their craving for the otherworldly energy that birthed them. There are five classifications of fae: alaeya, druin, golem, behemoth, and leviathan.

To summon magic, casters speak Words of power, calling on an aspect's true name to create spells. But all magic has a price. With each spell cast, the speaker accrues flux. Accrue too much and reality snaps back in strange and terrifying ways.

  • Master and combine Words to create powerful and unique spells
  • There are thousands of possible spell combinations
  • List of level one spells provided in the rulebook
  • Speak your spells using the constructed language Haretum 

THE CHRONICLER

The author of The Nuadan Chronicles is a mysterious figure within the world of Nuada. The Chronicler goes by many names and has many faces. Recently, they have been calling themself Seuma.

They wander about the world, appearing at important events or halls of research. Every few years, their latest manuscript and a handsome sum of money appear unexpectedly on the doorstep of a random publisher.

After many decades, the Chronicler has gained a cult following, worshippers claiming them to be an avatar of truth and knowledge or perhaps even the true god of this world. Efforts to ban the works have proven difficult due to their popularity.

Still, others suspect this strange figure, typically depicted in sensationalist exposés as a black-robed wanderer wielding a magic staff. What are the Chronicler's motives? Can their words be trusted? Who are they really?

 LIVING WORLD

We love games where your actions matter and can affect the outcome of a story. So we're making living storylines for the world of Nuada. Each campaign we release will feature a variety of outcomes as a result of players' decisions.

These decisions will be given a specific time frame to be submitted into an official survey. We tally the results and then announce the majority outcome. The campaign and its results then become canon, immortalized via free short stories and lore updates!

Your character's words and actions will have consequences for the wider world. Nations can fall. Allies can be won or lost. Important leaders and untold numbers of people can live or die.

We have already done two living storylines — The White Tower and The Haunted Forest — with playtesters. Those results and short stories will be featured in the lore book and on our website.

Many timelines lie in wait. But the past is set in stone. What is done cannot be undone. Seize the future — or someone else will.

 EXPLORATION

Much of the world is unmapped and unknown. There are hidden places in the wild forests, mountain valleys, and deep oceans. These places, such as the Valley of Demons in Merath or the ominous Red Line in Uldris, will be expounded upon in the lore book. Eventually, you will sail beyond the edges of the map to discover new lands and face unknown dangers and mysteries. What you find could change the world — for better or worse.

CONFLICT

Being a mercenary means you go looking for trouble. People always need someone to fight their wars, protect their wealth, or patrol a bawdy carnival. Whatever it is, you are in the thick of it — using your wits, charm or brute strength to live to see another day, hopefully with more money in your pockets than before.

In The Nuadan Chronicles, conflict can be straight-up fighting or more nuanced. You will see the frontlines of combat and the inner circles of the elite and powerful. Wage espionage, conduct investigations, protect sensitive diplomatic missions and hunt down wanted criminals.

 FATE

In a tabletop RPG where choices matter, you will struggle against destiny and the consequences of decisions made long before you took your first breath. There is a darkness in Nuada, older than the Lost Age, that infects the world even now. Ancient beings lurk behind the scenes, watching, waiting, scheming. They possess a terrible truth that could break the world — or set it free.

 DICE & MECHANICS

The Nuadan Chronicles uses a unique d100 system. "Bad" rolls do not always equal failure — the dice mechanics are weighted toward success. The more skilled a character is, the more likely they will succeed. Other dice are used to track supplies, but the main dice you will need are two d10s.

 CHARACTER CUSTOMIZATION

Players are free to design and grow their characters as they please. Explore and create new and interesting combinations through customized progression. 

  • Choose from nearly 40 backgrounds, each of which grants starting skills, gear, and unique traits.
  • Improve skills to increase your chances of success.
  • Use talents to further specialize your character and give them the edge.
  • Peruse an extensive list of weapons, armor, tech gear, and special items.
  • Dive into technomancy to upgrade items or study magic to do the impossible — or do both!
  • Complete your aesthetic and backstory by selecting a lineage, quirks, appearance, lore-friendly names, and important relationships.
  • Bonus: No levels! Instead, the amount of experience your character gains influences their threat rating — the more threatening you are, the more likely you will be targeted during a fight.

You might be wondering: Can I only play as a human in this game? Yes... for now. The outcomes of future campaigns may open up new possibilities!

 DYNAMIC COMBAT

Fighting is a grim, brutal business in Nuada. Players will need to be level-headed in how they approach situations — physical conflict is recommended as a last resort. When engaging the enemy, be strategic. A well-aimed blow can easily reduce a character's health to dangerously low levels. Combat features include:

  •  Narrative Round - Creates a smoother transition between narrative play and the rules of combat.
  •  Action Points - Spend AP to shoot, hit, run, grapple, etc., each round.
  •  Reactions - Dodge or parry blades, block bullets with your shield, or scatter from explosions.
  •  Locational Damage - Protect your health and stamina with armor.
  •  Power Hits - Hit an enemy hard enough to disorient, knock them down or dislodge a weapon from their grip.
  •  Limited Health - Be cautious — your health is finite and takes time to recover.
  •  Critical Hits - Deal a decisive blow for maximum damage.
  •  Death Points - Accrue enough damage and the character's pain will translate into penalties — gain too many and they may lose a limb or worse.
  •  And So Much More - Way more than we can fit on a Kickstarter page. Try the quickstart guide for a deeper look at the rules.
