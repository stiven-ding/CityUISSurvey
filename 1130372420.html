What is Quaggle?

Quaggle is a stacking, teetering, hanging, shifting, wobbling, unbalanced, balancing game that is a must have for friends and family game night! Take turns stacking the Quaggle pieces on the table top base. Each piece added can create a whole new shift of weight and balance, allowing for the sculpture to transform into something completely different. BEWARE, one wrong move could send it all crashing down!

This wooden toy is simple to play, tough to master, providing hours and hours of replay value especially due to the fact that you will never create two of the same structures. Quaggle is an adorable wooden balancing game for 1 to 12 players, All-natural wood pieces with bright, bold colors.

Game Overview

Quaggle comes with 4 different game modes to choose from so you can play at the pace and skill level you want. Below is a summarized form of each game mode.

1. Standard:

Players take turns trying to build the structure and earn points with each piece they place. Players strategically build over 1 or more rounds to see who can build the best and win the most points! This mode is great for players who enjoy a longer, at your own place, strategy game.

2. Chill:

Fun for family and friends at home or on the road, build interesting Quaggle Structures. No time requirement. No score keeping.

3. Countdown: “Quaggle Timer App” required (Available on App Store (iOS)/ Play Store (Android))

Players have a set time (Default 20 seconds) to place their piece. Any player who can't place their piece within the time or makes any piece fall or touch the table is out. The last player in the game wins. Great for players who like a fast paced game with high intensity.

4. Quick: “Quaggle Timer App” required (Available on App Store (iOS)/ Play Store (Android))

The App keeps track of each player's time to place their piece onto the structure for each round. Once all rounds are played, the player with the lowest time wins. Be careful not to make a piece fall or touch the table or you receive a penalty added to your time (Default 10 seconds)!

Risks and challenges

We went through 8 factories, 15 samples, more than 100 hours to put together the rules and over 8 months in order to get the correct wood, paint, size and overall concept that we wanted. Some of these challenges include 1. First time creating an App and not knowing the complexities therein 2. Lots of time investment which took away from other areas in my business 3. Challenge in creating these pieces, we thought it would be easy to cut and paint these shapes but it took 8 different factories in order to find on that can do it. 4. Had to find a sweet point in the size of the game relative to the retail cost We have done significant pre-production, details are constantly changing and the final product may not be identical to what you see above as those are prototypes. However, we have been doing this for 10 years and have made excellent relationships with manufacturers over the years; it was only a matter of time before we found the correct one. We've also spent months working on the physical design of the box and cards coupled with the logo and are confident we have a great final product that feels substantial and looks good both playing and sitting on your shelf.
