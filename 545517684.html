One is a skilled hunter, the eagle-eyed sniper who never misses a shot. The other is a fearsome werewolf, who is both a formidable villain and a literal party animal.

Presenting: The Red Dragon Inn Allies: Piper vs Ripsnarl!

You've seen these two tangle in Battle for Greyport, our cooperative deckbuilding game, but now they're coming to the tavern to join the party as our 48th and 49th characters for The Red Dragon Inn!

The Red Dragon Inn is a game for 2 or more with each player taking on the role of a heroic adventurer (or sometimes a dastardly villain) in a wild free-for-all party at a fantasy tavern. Brawl, drink and gamble the night away to prove your mettle and be the last player standing at the end of an evening of bad decision making. Will you be able to outplay each of your opponents? Or will you wake up the next morning with a throbbing headache, a bit less gold in your pocket, and a horse awkwardly nudging you out of his stall?

If this is your first time joining us, we suggest you check out our introduction to The Red Dragon Inn. And if you're new to the game, we have two pledge levels just for you: the Newcomer Edition, which includes a copy of the original Red Dragon Inn game, and the Newcomer Collector's Edition, which includes all the fancy swag plus the original Red Dragon Inn game. If you get one of the Newcomer Editions, you will have everything you need for a game of up to six players!

Risks and challenges

We pride ourselves in our ability to deliver a quality product on time, and we stand by our track record of doing so. However, here's a list of things you should know that could possibly go wrong: 1. Development Delay: Development on this project is nearly done! The only delay that could come here is if we notice something bad that we didn't see before. 2. Manufacturing Delay: We have a great relationship with our manufacturers and they work hard to get us our stuff on schedule. However, as always, unforeseen problems can arise. 3. Shipping Delay: Sometimes your stuff just goes missing and we have to print everything all over again. True story: an entire shipping container of our games was stolen back in July of 2012. Seriously. 4. Customs Delay: We have no control over the border policies of any country we import games into (including the US). Sometimes games get held up at a border - sometimes for a bafflingly long time. 5. Extraterrestrials could invade and enslave us to their will, and they may not be tabletop gamers. The good news is that this is not our first rodeo by a LONG shot. We have launched and delivered on multiple campaigns much to the satisfaction of our backers. We have padded our promised delivery date with all of the realistic delays in mind so you can confidently expect your game to arrive by our suggested delivery date.
