Do not hesitate and head out on a journey! Advantages of wind energy
Wind is used to produce electricity using the kinetic energy created by air in motion. Wind first hits a turbine’s blades, causing them to rotate and turn the turbine connected to them. That changes the kinetic energy to rotational energy, by moving a shaft which is connected to a generator, and thereby producing electrical energy through electromagnetism.

"An amazing thing about wind energy is that when the wind speed doubles, you can get eight times more power from a wind turbine. This is the cubic relationship of power to wind speed, which is why it can charge things so fast on a windy day."
- Wayne Groszko, Ph.D., Applied Energy Research Scientist

The cubic output of wind, paired with high-efficiency blades and lightweight design, allow the turbine to produce a large quantity of electricity.
