Story



On my travels from Ann Arbor Michigan across Land, Sea and Air I have discovered the amazing detailed crafting of bags and pouches from the land of Colombia in South America. Within that large country rest ranches which lives the Wayuu Indian tribe. In the Wayuu tribe the women craft the backpacks, pouches and Hammoks while the men craft belts and straps for the Backpacks. My friend Stephania revealed this knowledge after I knew her for a year, it was brought up when talking about crafts. I am an avid Card, Board and Role-Player whose interests are wide and varied. Seeing these bags they instantly reminded me of Dice Bags for gaming. 

Later down the road her family came to visit her and had the idea to set up a area in the local craft shops in Ibague, a large city near the Capital, And sell their generational crafts as their own business. I thought the idea was wonderful. Stephania came to me to ask if I knew a way to raise money for such a life changing endeavor. She needed money to buy the supplies and materials for the bags and to pay for shelving in the stores. I said ”Can you make Dice Bags”? She said certainly, She knew about as much about the gaming hobby as I had about Wayuu crafts, but I explained what they were used for and the game hobby and we were clicking and on the same page. 

So, here we are on the verge of not only offering a very unique and different item at the game table no one else will have, as well as a backstory of friendship, You will be changing someones life in the process. So, we have here a combination of a new endeavor with Stefania and her family bringing the background of bag makers and me from a gaming background crafting a new idea together. Lets get these made and have some fun at the table!
