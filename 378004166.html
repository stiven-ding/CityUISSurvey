Our story

Hello! We're Josh and Evan, the creators of Charty Party. If you're reading this and thinking about helping to bring Charty Party to life, you're most likely...

  • Someone looking for a fresh and hilarious party game
  • A huge nerd
  • One of our moms

Whether you've birthed us or not, we're super excited that you're as interested in creating absurd and ridiculous charts as we are. We played the first version of Charty Party on sticky notes at our little office conference table (we're coworkers at a tiny consulting firm), and when our coworker April couldn't stop laughing we knew we were onto something.

We're new to Kickstarter, but not new to launching and producing products, so we're excited to put our entrepreneurial experience into making sure everyone gets a beautifully printed game as smoothly as possible.

Of course, we can't do it without all of you! (No, seriously - we did the math and need at least 300 people to buy the game for us to afford the printing costs.) So, tell your friends, tell your grandma, and let's make this thing happen!

How to play

Risks and challenges

We'll come right out with it - we're not some big board game production company. We're just two dudes from Memphis with a love of charts. But, much like we'd say to the person sitting next to us the second time we attended a rodeo, "This isn't our first rodeo." Between the two of us we've overseen the launch of mobile apps, book releases, healthy drink products, and several non-profit organizations. We're no strangers to working with manufacturers, production schedules, and having a plan for when everything goes wrong all at once (it always does.) For Charty Party, we've already gotten prototypes printed, and have the manufacturing contracts in place with a reputable printing company to get started as soon as the campaign is complete. Of course, we can't promise we won't encounter some situation where we hit an unexpected delay. What we can promise, though, is that we'll be honest about any issues we encounter, and we're always just an email away if you have questions about the status of the campaign.
