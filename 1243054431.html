Broken!  A KickStarter campaign like none before.  

We're creating a role playing game.  A game designed based on choices our backers vote for.  Backers will decide the game mechanic, genre and races. 

Keep reading, support the campaign and buckle up because its going to be a wild ride!

What the H*LL?!?! 

The fine folks at KickStarter asked for people to create new types of campaigns as part of the "Break KickStarter" promotion so we're doing just that! 

This is more than the game development and art, this is a month long social event!  Take to social media and advocate for your choices. Convince others to pledge and cast their votes to support what you want Broken! to be. You can only pledge/vote once but don't let that stop you from swaying others to join the campaign and help us to create a truly unique RPG.  Let's get everyone involved!!

How does this work?

For starters, everyone pledging at a tier gets the game.  We'll push out play test version first (digital, PDF) by December of this year and revise as needed to arrive at the completed game (estimated to be done by July 2020).  

Once the play test period is over, the game content will get revised into finished product stage.  All backers will receive their PDF for that version and print on demand codes for use through drivethrurpg.com so you can order a print copy.  The print on demand code will allow you to purchase the book at the print on demand print cost (same price I pay to have them printed through drivethrurpg) plus your shipping.  

How are you going to make this?

This is going to be the hardest bit of game development I've ever done.  Here's how its going to work, so pay attention everyone, this is how the decisions are made and Broken! gets created.

Phase One 

You pick the tier you want to pledge to. We have three categories of voting tiers: Tiers that reflect what game mechanic, tiers that reflect genre and tiers that reflect what types of races we're incorporating.  Pledge at the tier that is most important to you in the game's development.

Phase Two :

Advocate for your choice. This is how Broken! will get funded and made.  We're calling on all backers to take to social media (don't pay for ads or boosting posts, we're just talking about normal posts/tweets/etc)  and tell folks why your choice is right for what Broken! will become.   Spread the word and your own influence to help make Broken! the RPG that truly represents what the TTRPG community wants.

During the campaign I (W.S. "Sam" Quinton... you know, the guy in the video) will be frequently live streaming to promote this campaign.  I invite you to do the same and, if you do, invite me on and if I can make it I will!  Note that if multiple people request the same date/time the first person requesting will have priority regardless of how big your audience is.  

Phase Three :

Step One:

When the campaign ends we'll tally the votes.  The tier with the most pledges (votes) will determine which tier choice is used in game development.  {Example:  If 2000 people pledge for 5E OGL game mechanic and 2 pledge for M.A.R.S then 5E will be the game mechanic used in the game}. 

Once the campaign is done we'll begin the design and release our play test version in PDF to all backers (Estimated release of play test version is December 2019).  Play the game, tell us what is good and what sucks.  Post comments here on the page so we (and the whole world) can see them.  We'll take that information and refine the content into the final version of the game.  The PDF file will be updated along the way as we work toward a final version.

Step Two:

Once the final version is done, we'll update the PDF release to the final version which means you'll get it automatically through drivethrurpg.   We'll then release the print on demand codes here for all backers.  There will be two codes released (one for soft cover and one for hard cover) and each will be valid for all backers.

Where does the money go?

Development 90%: Money raised from this campaign will be used to pay artists, writers, art director and editor.  This campaign has been streamlined to pay for creation, while paying a fair wage to folks working on the project.  Backer rewards are easy to fulfill and forego charging for shipping during the KickStarter campaign which helps to keep our overall goal lower.

KickStarter fees/copyright/legal expenses 10%: The mundane part of game development. 

These figures have been rounded for simplicity.

Risks and challenges

Developing a game scratch based on the popular vote for key elements is as difficult an undertaking as I can conceive of in game design. Really, there are a number of things that can cause problems for this project. So lets look at what we can do to make things run as smoothly as possible. I've run and fulfilled successful campaigns before and I'm confident that we've identified some key risks. 1) Writing ~ quality writing takes time. If contributing writers miss deadlines we will see delays. For this purpose I'm doing most of the writing myself and commissioning writing from industry writers known to me. 2) Art delays ~ If something happens to delay Zack or Lotus in art development it will work against the timeline. Zack and Lotus are the principle artists on this project. If need be we can bring on other artists for illustrations to be included in meeting Stretch Goals. To be honest though, I've NEVER had either of them miss a deadline. Ever. 3) Delayed response to contribution inquiries and backer surveys. This is a big one! We'll be collecting data very quickly so as to begin work on the project. Don't delay, answer your backer survey quickly so we can get your information in for PDF distribution so you can be playing Broken! when the play test version first goes out! 4) Participation: This campaign is dependent upon backer involvement. We not only want your feedback after the play test version goes out, we really and truly NEED it! Please post comments to the main campaign page addressing any concerns, game play problems or other "this sucks" kind of development problems. We'll work to fix the problems in Broken! before final release in order to create the best game possible.
