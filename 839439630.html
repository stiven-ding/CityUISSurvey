Story

We're taking fan film-making to the next level.  The Holocron is a short Star Wars fan film that has been produced by a team of professional filmmakers in collaboration with film school students.  We're showing them how movies are shot, and having fun doing it!  I mean, who doesn't like Star Wars?

 What are holocrons anyway? They are data artifacts that store the knowledge of the Jedi or Sith who created them (for better or worse.) Stumbling across one can open doors to a greater understanding of the Force…or allow the darkness to come pouring in.  Our story follows Eramin, a supposed Jedi Master who hunts holocrons and races against the Sith Lord Corvus for ownership of these dangerous prizes. But this latest holocron is different - the soul of a rather cunning and manipulative Sith Mistress resides deep within, and Eramin is about to learn what happens when he falls prey to her dark whisperings.

Holocrons have yet to be shown on the silver screen, though they have played a part in the animated series Star Wars Rebels and Attack of the Clones. We think it would be pretty cool to show Star Wars fans what a real-life holocron looks like, and how it might behave if it falls into the wrong hands.

We’re a bunch of Star Wars fans, but we’re also professional filmmakers. And we think you’ll enjoy watching our fan short film as much as we’ll enjoy making it. We're challenging ourselves to see if we can shoot a film that looks nearly identical to a massive budget, studio-funded one.

But more importantly, our Director is a professor of film at the University of Colorado Denver, and this is a learning opportunity for her students! How do Hollywood, big-budget films get made? How did they shoot that lightsaber fight scene in the forest? What is the process of casting actors, scouting locations, and assembling props and costumes? How do you use industry-standard gear, like jibs and steadicams?

These are skills and answers that the film crew (which consists mostly of students) will learn through the process of making this 10-minute short. Key positions will be held by industry professionals who have shot features, corporate, commercials, and documentaries world-wide. This is a chance for students to get hands-on training with experienced filmmakers to learn directly under them, without having to pay for an expensive internship. By the time they graduate, they will have The Holocron as a portfolio piece. 

Our team consists of industry professionals holding the top tier crew positions, with very talented film students from CU Denver filling in the remaining and secondary roles.  Students will get to shadow the pros to learn proper on-set techniques (and play with some really sweet gear).  We are diverse, hard-working, and passionate Star Wars fans!

Risks and challenges

Film making is always risky, because it hinges on a lot of factors (like infamous Colorado weather, unexpected budget changes, and availability of crew/actors). But the good news is, we're pros who do this everyday for our jobs. We know how to spend money wisely, schedule properly, and run a crew. If not, we're good at adapting and rolling with the punches. That's just part of working in the film industry. We're confident we will deliver a fantastic Star Wars short film on time and on budget.
