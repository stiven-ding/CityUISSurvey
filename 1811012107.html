Story

My name is Tyler Hurdle, and I am an independent game developer who has dedicated the past 3 years to developing physics, character controls, and scripted events inside of other video games using Unreal Engine. After a long hiatus of my own ideas, I came up with the idea of Electropelago after a trip to the East coast. The ocean struck me immediately through its vastness and the amount of life that thrives in its unforgiving waters, and I want to convey that same feeling inside of a playable adventure for PC.

I'd be lying if I said TLOZ:Windwaker does not inspire me, so I'll say it. 

Windwaker inspired this game just as much as my visit to the East coast. But even though the inspirations are there, this is not a clone, and at the end of the day my goal is to have many players experience this game and walk away with the idea the inspirations for Electropelago came from nothing but the ocean. 

Lighthouses, tropical vegetation, sandy beaches, chill islanders, open skies, stormy seas and upgradeable boats will all accompany this game for a full, exploration-heavy experience that will end whenever the player wants it to end. The idea of this game is not to force a story on to the player, but simply to carry on the story until you, the player, make your way onto the farthest island in the archipelago. 

You can sail to the final island as soon as the game starts, effectively ending the game in less than an hour, or explore the hundreds of islands in the archipelago to find hidden collectible items, chat with the islanders, and upgrade your ship using parts found scattered throughout the islands. 


Saving throughout the game is handled through a battery system. If you do not charge your batteries using the charging station placed at any island within three in-game days, your batteries will run out, and you will "die", effectively ending the game, and sending you back to the last charging station you activated.

The choice will be yours, and will be as vast as the ocean I want to create. 

Risks and challenges

The largest risk of this project is the fact that I am an independent developer, and not many people would like to contribute to a project knowingly when that fact is present. However, I fully intend to see the development of Electropelago through to the end, and I would love your help in any contribution, big or small, to help alleviate a small amount of the financial stress of developing a video game on my own. Your contributions will be extremely appreciated, and for anyone who contributes, your name will be placed in the credits of the game and you will all receive an invite to a Discord channel where I will visit regularly to give updates and receive feedback from the people who want to see this through!
