Story

Several years ago RJ Tomlinson developed a sketchbook that helped him in his design process of producing playing cards. The original sketchbook was smaller in size. This sketchbook was funded on Kickstarter and received positive reviews but also feedback for improvements.  

With the advice of fellow playing card designer, Lance Miller, RJ made some improvements to the original product. Now the sketchbook is bigger and on better quality drawing paper.

The Playing Card Designer sketchbook and pad is a simple but very useful tool for putting your dreams on paper. For any designer the worst part of designing playing cards is the tedious task of creating a template that allows you to create intricate symmetrical work. Now with the Playing Card Designer sketchbook and pad you can start designing immediately. 

Why a sketchbook?

For this product, necessity is the mother of invention. Playing Card designer and producer, RJ Tomlinson wanted a sketchbook to have around at all times for committing ideas to paper. Since there isn't any sketchbooks around for this use we decided to create one. With the playing card community so active here on Kickstarter we decided to share our sketchbook with all.

The Paper

We tested dozens of premium paper to find one that gave use the performance we were looking for. We wanted something that would work well with pens, markers and pencils.

The Inside Pages

Inside we have several differnt page layouts, all seperated with colored divider pages. These page layouts include a large full sheet page design, quarter pages with actual card sizes, tuck case layout pages and pip layout pages.

The inside pages are printed with 100% cyan ink. This allows artists to easily scan the artwork into Photoshop and extract the cyan grids thus leaving only the artwork.
