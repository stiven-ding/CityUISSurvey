Story

I've run a few games like this. I think a lot of us have. You have a specific adventure in mind, but you aren't sure how to do it - what system to use, what sort-of characters. So you pick a system that you're familiar with and force the adventure into that mold. And that works okay. But once in a while you say F it and write up your own thing: your own set of rules for the weird little side-trip into fantasy land that you want to make. And maybe that works better, or maybe not, but you remember what fun it was. 

I've done that more times than I can count. Rare Birds is one of them. It's an effort to make a really flexible system with guideposts that help the storyteller/GM tell the story. And it gives a player with even very limited gaming experience a number of handles on their character, in plain language. There are also some optional player-side narrative tools. 

I'm taking the games I've run like that, in that system, and I'm putting them together for you. See what you think.  

Risks and challenges

I have some design choices to make. I've run this system using different number scales in the past, and I'll need to narrow things down and exclude a few things in order to present a sensible whole. Sometimes editing things down is tough for me. But I can do it! This isn't a huge project. It's just a few pages of rules, some characters, and a scenario. That's the sort of thing I routinely do for a convention or a group of friends in a couple of weeks.
